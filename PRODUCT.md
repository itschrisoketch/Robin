# PRODUCT.md — Robin

**Register:** product (an app UI — a chat tool that serves the contributor)

## Product purpose
Robin is a context-aware AI guide for prospective Bitcoin open-source contributors. It tells them **what to work on**, **what to read first**, and — the thesis — **when not to contribute yet**. It attacks the problem one layer upstream of the "PR filter" everyone else builds: it prevents the low-context PR from being written by giving contributors the context they're missing.

The signature behavior is **honest redirection**: when someone with no relevant background asks "what should I work on in Bitcoin Core?", Robin says *"Not Core. Not yet."* and points them to smaller projects where they'll genuinely help. An AI tool that turns people away from prestige in service of the ecosystem.

## Users
- **The bootcamp grad** — three months of Rust, finished a tutorial, excited, picked Bitcoin Core because it's famous. Nervous, eager, no context. Needs to feel guided, not judged.
- **The senior engineer** — deep C++/consensus background, limited time, wants to land something that matters. Needs signal on what maintainers are focused on right now.

## Tone
Warm, mentorly, honest. A trusted trail guide, not a gatekeeper. Robin's closing thesis: *"Open source isn't a gate. It's a path. We built the signs."* Never condescending; redirection is generous, not a rejection.

## Brand metaphor
**The field guide / wayfinding.** Trail markers, signposts, a compass. Robin guides you down the right path. The redirection is a fork in the trail, marked kindly.

## Anti-references (what to avoid)
- Crypto-terminal cliché: neon on black, Bitcoin-orange gradients, ticker aesthetics.
- "Bouncer" / error / rejection framing for redirection.
- Generic SaaS chat (Inter, gray bubbles, purple gradient).
- Anything that reads as "AI made that."

## Strategic principles
- The two-persona demo is the entire product. Every choice serves that 3-minute moment.
- Demo reliability over everything. Scripted golden path; never depend on conference wifi.
- Honest redirection must feel intentional and warm, visually distinct from a normal answer.
